Getting Here

The Cowichan Valley can be accessed by car, boat or air. Travel to Vancouver Island using BC Ferries to Swartz Bay or Nanaimo, or use the Black Ball ferry to Victoria.

Via Tsawwassen, south of Vancouver

Directions from Canadian/US border 
Take highway 17 south from highway 99 to the BC Ferry Tsawwassen Terminal. Pay fare for travel to Long Harbour, (1.5 hours), Swartz Bay, (1.5 hours), or to Duke Point, (2 hours). Travel through Long Harbour will require a second ferry trip at Vesuvius, (34 minutes).

Via Victoria

At Port Angeles, Washington, pay fare for travel to Victoria, BC via Black Ball Ferry. The Cowichan Valley is located about 50km, (30 miles), north of Victoria.

Via Horseshoe Bay, Northwest of Vancouver, BC

At BC Ferry Terminal, pay fare for travel to Nanaimo, BC.

Marine Traffic

Travel by pleasure craft to local marinas at Mill Bay, Cowichan Bay, Maple Bay, or Chemainus.

Air Traffic

The Cowichan Valley is served by a private air field operated by the Duncan Flying Club. Scheduled air service is available to Victoria or Nanaimo daily.
